# Event-Driven Deep Research Reference

## Academic Research: Pre-FOMC Drift

Federal Reserve Bank of New York Staff Report 512 (2015):
- "Since the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) began announcing its policy decisions in 1994, U.S. stock returns have on average been more than thirty times larger on announcement days than on other days."
- "The excess returns earned during the twenty-four hours prior to scheduled FOMC announcements account for more than 80 percent of the equity premium over the past seventeen years."
- Verified persistent through 2024 by multiple independent researchers.

## Historical Event Case Studies

### Iran Strike — February 2026
- Algorithms processed war news and sold within 400 milliseconds
- Oil +7% immediately
- Normalization within weeks per historical pattern
- **Lesson**: Algos react faster than humans. Do not try to out-speed them.

### Trump Tariff Pause — April 2025
- Social media post at 1:18 PM ET
- S&P +9.5% in hours
- **Lesson**: Unscheduled political events create the largest, fastest moves. Have a protocol.

### PPI Miss — March 2026
- Headline 0.5% vs 1.1% expected
- Immediate relief rally: Nasdaq +1.96%, S&P 500 +1.18%
- **Lesson**: Inflation data surprises have outsized impact in current regime.

## Flash Crash Protection

### Protocol
1. Always use hard stops (not mental stops)
2. Maintain 20-30% cash buffer for margin expansion
3. Know your broker's circuit breaker rules
4. Never trade with maximum margin during high-vol periods
5. Have emergency close procedure if platform freezes

### Historical Flash Crashes
- May 6, 2010: Dow fell 1,000 points in minutes, recovered most same day
- August 24, 2015: S&P 500 down 5% at open, circuit breakers triggered
- March 2020: Multiple circuit breaker halts during COVID crash

## Unscheduled Event Protocol

### Immediate Response (First 5 Minutes)
1. Check if your positions are affected
2. Assess if stop would trigger on normal volatility
3. If volatility >3x normal: consider manual exit
4. Document the event for later review

### The 4-Hour Rule
Most geopolitical shocks normalize within 4 hours:
- Initial algo-driven overreaction
- Human traders assess actual impact
- Reversion toward pre-event levels
- Exception: wars with energy market implications (oil spike persists)
