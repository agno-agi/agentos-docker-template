# Event-Driven Trading Skill

Economic releases create predictable volatility windows. The principle: **trade the reaction, not the release.** Most retail traders lose during events due to slippage, spread widening, and emotional overreaction. This skill provides specific playbooks for each major event type.

## Economic Event Impact Hierarchy

### Tier 1 — Extreme Impact (Specialize or Avoid)
| Event | Schedule | Typical Move | Trading Approach |
|-------|----------|--------------|------------------|
| **NFP** | First Friday, 8:30 AM | NQ 100-300 pts | Pre-position or post-momentum |

## The 3-Phase Event Pattern

Most Tier 1 events follow this sequence:

**Phase 1: Pre-Event Positioning (30-60 min before)**
- Volatility contracts as participants wait
- Range-bound behavior, tighter ranges
- Pre-FOMC drift: 80% of equity premium since 1994 occurs in 24h before announcement

**Phase 2: The Release (0-5 min after)**
- Massive volatility spike
- Slippage 2-5x normal
- Spreads widen dramatically
- **DO NOT TRADE THIS PHASE**

**Phase 3: The Reaction (5-60 min after)**
- Dust settles, directional commitment forms
- Most predictable phase for retail traders
- Two sub-patterns: follow-through or fade

## Event-Specific Playbooks

### NFP Playbook (First Friday, 8:30 AM)

**Option A: Pre-Positioning**
- Enter 30 minutes before release in direction of trend
- Size: 50% normal
- Stop: Wide (2x normal)
- Target: 1R only, no runners
- **Risk**: Slippage on stop if wrong direction

**Option B: Wait-for-Dust-to-Settle**
- Do not trade 8:30-8:35 AM
- At 8:35, assess initial direction
- If strong directional candle with volume: enter in that direction
- Stop: Below the 8:35 candle low/high
- Target: 1.5-2R
- **Higher WR, lower stress**

**Option C: Fade the Spike**
- Wait for initial spike in one direction
- Look for reversal candle at key level
- Enter fade with tight stop
- **Best when**: NFP print is inline with expectations (no surprise)

### FOMC Playbook (8x/year, 2:00 PM + 2:30 PM)

**The Pre-FOMC Drift (Academically Validated)**
- Federal Reserve Bank of New York: pre-FOMC returns account for >80% of equity premium since 1994
- Excess returns 30x larger on announcement days
- **Implementation**: Maintain baseline long exposure into FOMC when macro supports it

**The Dual-Window Strategy**
- **Window 1 (2:00 PM)**: Statement release. Initial volatility 2:00-2:05 PM. Avoid.
- **Window 2 (2:30 PM)**: Press conference. Often creates MORE volatility than statement.
- **Trade**: Directional momentum 2:35-3:30 PM if clear trend forms.

**Recent Caveat**: BTC fell after 7 of 8 FOMC meetings in 2025, even during rate cuts. "Sell the news" pattern has dominated.

### CPI Playbook (Monthly, 8:30 AM)

**Key Statistic**: Edgeful data — 80% of green initial CPI reactions led to RED CLOSES on YM over 1-year lookback.

**Playbook**:
- Do NOT chase initial direction
- Wait 15-20 minutes for dust to settle
- If initial green reaction: prepare fade (short bias)
- If initial red reaction: often continues (follow-through)
- **Reason**: CPI surprises create overreaction that corrects

### EIA Oil Report (Wednesdays, 10:30 AM)

**The 65-70% Reversal Pattern**
- Initial reaction in direction of inventory surprise
- 30-45 minutes after release: reversal begins
- **Trade**: Fade the initial reaction at 10:45-11:00 AM
- Stop: Beyond the 10:30 AM extreme
- Target: Pre-release level or VWAP

## Risk Management for Events

### Before Known Events
- Reduce position size 30-50%
- Widen stops 1.5-2x normal
- Consider going flat 5 minutes before release
- Use LIMIT ORDERS only (no market orders during events)

### During Events
- 5-minute no-trade window around release
- Monitor slippage — if >2 ticks, stop trading
- Watch for platform freezes (common during NFP)

### After Events
- Wait for 2 consecutive 1-min candles in same direction
- Require volume expansion (RVOL > 1.5)
- Return to normal size once trend is clear

## Earnings Season Impact on Futures

### NQ Sensitivity
- 60% of NQ concentrated in 7 tech stocks
- AAPL, MSFT, NVDA, AMZN, GOOGL, META, TSLA
- Earnings weeks: ATR expands 200-400 points
- Volume increases 25-35%

### Trading Approach
- Pre-earnings: position for volatility expansion (long strangle bias)
- Post-earnings: trade the momentum continuation or gap fill
- Earnings calendar: know dates for all 7 major components
